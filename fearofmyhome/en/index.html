Fear of my Home

A system about fear, anguish, and survival within The Home. Run.

Chapter 0: Introduction

When everything first became far too quiet,
I thought it was just the house sleeping.
I used to imagine the walls were hollow,
that behind them there was nothing but empty space.

If I stayed quiet enough,
maybe I could pass through it all like smoke.
It was comforting to think that way.
All I had to do was believe nothing truly held me.

But then I grew up,
and learned what lives inside the walls.
I learned why they creak,
why they seem to move closer when no one is looking.

Today, I no longer try to pass through.
Today, I stand still.
And wait for the house to pass through me.

The Home

No one knows when The Home began. Perhaps it has always existed. It manifests wherever fear meets silence. And it feeds on what is left behind. During sleep, defenses fall. That is when The Home opens. Every child passes through it. Not all of them remember. What lives in the wardrobe did not come from a dream. What hides under the bed does not leave when the lights turn on. And whatever smiles too much has learned how to imitate human faces. Run.

Chapter 1: Character Creation

Attributes

Characters in Fear of My Home have three attributes: Physical (PHY), Mind (MND), and Instinct (INS). During character creation, choose one attribute to receive 1 point, one to receive 2, and one to receive 3. After that, you may add +1 point to any attribute.

Luca is an athletic boy with very sharp senses. He chose 3 points in Physical, 2 in Instinct, and 1 in Mind. He then added +1 to Instinct, ending with PHY 3, MND 1, INS 3.

Skills

While attributes indicate raw potential, skills show where the character can act with precision. All skills start at 1. During creation, distribute 6 additional points. At most two skills may reach 3 points, and none may have 4 or more.

Concepts

Concepts are short phrases that describe something defining about the character: a physical trait, an item, a habit, a reputation, a trauma, or a social condition. You must create four concepts. Each one must have both a positive and a negative interpretation.

James has worn glasses since childhood and feels more focused when thinking carefully. Noah also wears glasses, but at school it became a constant target for mockery. While James may receive a +1 bonus to Knowledge, Noah may receive -1 to Connect, even though both chose the concept “Glasses.”

Chapter 2: Tests

Tests

A test occurs whenever an action’s outcome may vary. If there are multiple possible consequences, dice determine the result. If there is no meaningful risk, the GM may allow the action without a roll.

Rolls

Rolls use six-sided dice (d6) and are tied to an attribute and a skill. Roll a number of d6 equal to attribute + skill. Then, keep a number of dice equal to the skill and sum them. Compare the result to the Test Difficulty (TD).

Difficulty Trivial Easy Medium Hard Very Hard Nearly Impossible
TD Value 6 9 12 15 18 21

Conflicts

Conflicts are tests where the difficulty is the opponent’s roll. When two characters oppose each other, the highest result wins.
